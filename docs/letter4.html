Letter 4 - August 17th

"Edale",
2, Cranbourne Road,
Chortlton-cum-Hardy,
Manchester, 21,
17/8/42.

My dear Jim,

Very many thanks for your
letter, which I was pleased to
receive. I am simply delighted
to think that our friendship is
still going to continue, and
I hope we shall have very
many happy times together.

It was good of you to spare
a few minutes to write to me
on Sunday, as I know how
busy you must have been,
and I do appreciate it Jim.
It is Morris’ last Sunday next
week.

2.

Our new minister, who is coming
in September, the one from
Headingley College, is named
“Rees”. Dad had a letter from
him, and I understand he
lives in Cardiff.

I think Margaret and I
are going to the Paramount to see
“How Green was my Valley”, as
it is supposed to be such a
good film.

On Wednesday, I am going
with the Platt Lane League e
to Hartley College, where some
of them are playing in a
tennis tournament. Needless
to say, I am not playing tennis.

3.

It would certainly have been
lovely if we could have had
our full fortnight’s holiday
together Jim, but still it
was grand to have had a
week, wasn’t it.

I am so glad you like
Mother and Dad, as I am sure
they will always make you very
welcome. Mother suggested that
on Saturday, we may as well
come back hometo tea, as it
would be better than having
to get it out somewhere.

Perhaps you would like me
to show you where Platt Church
is, as it would be a nice walk &

4.

a friend of mine - Daisy
Harding - from church, has
gone to Dhalling Mhor for this
week, so I suppose she will
have a great time if the
weather improves. She has
joined up in the NAAFI’s, and
is stationed in Gloucester at
present.

I shall be looking forward
to seeing you again on Saturday,
at 3-oclock.

Kindest regards,
Yours very sincerely,
Doreen.
