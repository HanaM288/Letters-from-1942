Letter 10 - September 1st

"Edale",
2, Cranbourne Road,
Chortlton.cum.Hardy,
Manchester, 21.
1st Sept., 1942

My dear Jim,

It was simply wonderful to
receive your letter this morning.
I am so happy to think that we
have so much in common. I do
love you with all my heart Jim,
and I am so delighted to know
that you feel the same towards me.
I am always thinking about you,
and I am so proud to think
you are doing such a great
work, which is the greatest
thing in life.

2.



This weekend will for ever
live in my memory, dear.
I enjoyed evey minute of it.
It was funny you should mention
hymn 443, as I was thinking
about that on Sunday. I remember
once learning all the words
off by heart. It is such a
beautiful hymn, and can express
one’s feelings far better than
words could ever do.

I am sure we shall have such
wonderful times together, Jim.

It seems so strange that we
should live so near, and yet
have to meet so far away as
Scotland, but I am sure there

3.



must be some divine purpose
behind it all.

Do let me know what size
gaiters you want. They have
them in 26” - 30” length.

I called in to-day for your
book, but it hadn’t come in,
so I will go again later in the week.

We had a grand swim
yesterday, although it was a
bit cold on first getting in.

I am so much looking forward
to seeing you on Saturday.

By the way, if you have any
good ideas as to how we

4.



can entertain our guests the
following Saturday please let
me know, as I am trying to
plan my programme.

I do love you more than
anything else Jim; do believe
this, from the bottom
of my heart.

all my good wishes,
Yours for always,
Doreen.
