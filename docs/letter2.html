Letter 2 - Tuesday

"Edale",
2, Cranbourne Road,
Chortlton-cum-Hardy,
Manchester, 21,
Tuesday.

My dear Jim,

Thank you ever so much for the
nice letter which you sent me. It
was good of you to write so soon.
I, too, must say that it has been
the most delightful holiday I have
ever spent, and would express
my sincere thanks for the very
happy times which we had
together.

I have been into the office,
and have now got settled down
somewhat, but all the same,
I can’t help wishing I was
back at Dhalling Mhor!

2.

We were late arriving in Manchester
on Saturday. We got in about 7.0.
instead of 5.0. You were quite
right in supposing that it would
be raining. It certainly was, and
I do not think it has stopped
since.

I received Poppy’s letter here,
and have forwarded it on to
her, thank you.

I am sending you a photograph,
which was recently taken, if you
care to have it, as I think it is
a bit better than the one we
had taken at Dunoon

Enclosed you will find my £ 3.,
as I cannot spend them here
without losing on them (I have
cultivated the Scotch habit, haven’t I?)

3.

Perhaps we could have the
21st “do”on the 12th September, if
this will be convenient to you.
However, let me know, and then
we can fix it.

If ever you are in Manchester
before then, do not hesitate to go
into the Central Hall and see
Dad, as I am sure he will
be pleased to meet you.

I have been looking at the
photograph of Swanwick - 1939 -
and notice both you and Dad
on it, so, no doubt, you
will recognise him when
you see him.

4.

Well Jim! I do hope you
have a really good holiday, and
that you will feel much better
for it when you come back.

I trust the weather will improve.
Please give my kind regards
to Jack and Jean, Harry & Ellie,
and, of course, John. Do not stay
awake talking all night, will you?

Dad was enquiring about Ruby,
and they said she will have
to see a specialist, as it will
probably mean an operation.

All the very best, and
hoping to see you soon.
Yours very sincerely,
Doreen
